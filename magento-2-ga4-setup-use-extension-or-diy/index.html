Magento 2 GA4 Setup: Use Extension or DIY?

Published on: August 23, 2024

Google Analytics 4 (GA4) is the latest evolution in web analytics, offering crucial insights for eCommerce businesses. For Magento 2 store owners, implementing GA4 is essential to leverage its advanced tracking capabilities and make data-driven decisions. However, when setting up GA4 for Magento 2, owners face a key decision: use a pre-built extension or opt for a do-it-yourself (DIY) approach. This choice involves considering factors such as technical expertise, budget, customization needs, and maintenance. Both options have merits and drawbacks, making it crucial to evaluate which method best suits your store’s requirements. Key considerations include integration complexity, custom event tracking needs, and eCommerce feature compatibility.

Understanding Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

What is GA4?

GA4 is Google’s next-generation analytics platform, designed to provide a more comprehensive view of user interactions across websites and apps. It differs significantly from Universal Analytics (UA) in several key ways:

  1. Event-based tracking: GA4 uses an event-based data model, allowing for more flexible and detailed tracking of user interactions. This model enables businesses to create custom events without additional coding, providing richer insights into user behavior.
  2. Enhanced measurement: GA4 automatically tracks common events like scrolls, outbound clicks, and file downloads. This out-of-the-box functionality offers more comprehensive tracking capabilities with minimal setup.
  3. Cross-device tracking: Unlike UA’s session-based approach, GA4 provides a unified view of user journeys across multiple devices and platforms. This feature improves understanding of multi-touch attribution and provides a more accurate picture of the customer journey.
  4. AI-powered insights: GA4 leverages machine learning to offer predictive metrics and anomaly detection, helping businesses anticipate user behavior and identify trends.
  5. Improved privacy controls: Designed to function without cookies and with limited use of IP addresses, GA4 helps businesses comply with evolving privacy regulations.

Why Magento 2 Stores Need GA4

  1. Enhanced tracking of user behavior and conversions:
    • GA4’s event-based model allows for more precise tracking of eCommerce-specific actions, such as product views, add-to-carts, and purchases.
    • Custom events can be easily created to track Magento 2 GA4 specific interactions, providing deeper insights into user engagement.
    • Cross-device tracking helps understand how customers interact with the store across different devices before making a purchase.
  1. Improved data for decision-making and marketing optimization:
    • GA4’s advanced analysis tools, including funnel analysis and path exploration, help identify bottlenecks in the conversion process specific to Magento 2 stores.
    • Predictive metrics can forecast potential revenue and identify high-value customers, allowing for more targeted marketing efforts.
    • Seamless integration with Google’s advertising platforms enables more effective audience targeting and campaign optimization for Magento 2 stores.
    • Enhanced eCommerce reporting provides detailed insights into product performance, helping inform inventory and pricing decisions.
    • AI-driven insights can uncover trends and patterns in customer behavior, enabling data-driven improvements to the Magento 2 store’s user experience and marketing strategies.

How to Set Up Google Analytics 4 in Magento 2?

1. Set Up a GA4 Property

Before adding GA4 to your Magento store, you’ll need to set up a GA4 property. If you already have one, you can skip to the next step.

To create a GA4 property, go to Google Analytics and click on “Get started today” to begin the setup process. You’ll need to provide your Account Name and choose your Account Data Sharing Settings.

Next, proceed to the Property setup. Enter the property name and fill in additional details, such as the reporting time zone and preferred currency.

After that, you’ll need to provide details about your business, including its category, size, and purpose. This information helps Google Analytics customize your experience accordingly.

After accepting the Google Analytics Terms, you’re nearly finished with the setup. The final step is to create a data stream, which tells GA4 where to track your data. Choose “Web” as your platform.

Next, enter your website URL and assign a Stream name. Be sure to click the “Create stream” button, and that’s it—you’ve successfully set up your Google Analytics 4 property.

2. Get your Measurement ID

After creating a GA4 property, you’ll be taken to the Web stream details page, where you can find the Measurement ID that you’ll need later.

If your property is already set up, navigate to your Google Analytics account, go to Admin > Data Streams, and select the relevant data stream to retrieve the Measurement ID.

3. Set Up Google Tag Manager Account and Container

Since we’ll be adding Google Analytics 4 to Magento through Google Tag Manager, you’ll need to set up GTM as well. If you’ve already created your GTM account, feel free to skip to the next step.

To set up GTM, go to the homepage and click the “Create Account” button. Enter the Account Name, select your Country, and specify the Container Name. Then, choose your target platform.

Note: The container name is a descriptive label that helps you identify and link the container to your website.

4. Get your Public ID, Account ID and Container ID

In addition to the Google Analytics Measurement ID, you’ll also need to obtain tracking IDs from GTM. Navigate to the GTM homepage and select the container you plan to use for tracking.

  • Public ID 

Go to the Admin tab to locate the public ID next to the container name.

  • Account ID

You can find the account ID on the container page when you’re in the Workspace tab. It is in the browser address after /accounts/.

  • Container ID

The container ID can also be found in the container page URL, following /containers/.

5. Install Magento 2 Google Analytics 4 Extension

As previously noted, integrating Google Analytics 4 with Magento is significantly easier and less technical when using the right solution. The next step is to install the Magento 2 Google Analytics 4 extension and manage it through the admin panel.

6. Paste your GTM and GA4 IDs into Magento Admin

After installing the GA4 extension, navigate to Stores > Configuration > Meetanshi Extensions > Google Tag Manager and Analytics > Web Container. Then, paste the GTM tracking IDs you collected earlier.

Note: If you’re using Stape.io or another custom GTM loader, select “Head Script” and “Body Noscript” in the Installation Method field.

Next, expand the Google Analytics 4 section and paste your Measurement ID. Be sure to save your settings.

7. Generate GTM Tags

You can manually create custom events such as add_to_cart or initiate_checkout in your GTM account if desired. However, if you have the Google Analytics 4 extension, this step is not necessary.

While on the extension configuration page, scroll down to the Export Web Container section and click the “Generate JSON Container & Download File” button to obtain a file with all the essential tags.

Once the file is downloaded, go to the GTM homepage, click on Admin, and select the Import Container option.

Select the file to import, choose the workspace, and decide whether to overwrite or merge the selected workspace with the container.

Note: If you choose “Existing Workspace,” select it from the list (typically the default option).

After selecting a workspace, you’ll be prompted to confirm the imported tags, triggers, and variables. Once confirmed, you will need to verify the GTM connection.

8. Test the GTM Connection

GTM lets you verify if Google Tag Manager is functioning correctly directly from the workspace. On the container page workspace, click Preview and enter your website URL in the popup that appears.

The URL will open in a new browser tab, and you’ll see a success message on the GTM page. Click Continue to view all monitored tags in the Tag Assistant.

Here’s also where you can test the events by triggering them on your website.

9. Check Google Analytics 4 in Realtime

If the GTM tags are functioning correctly, they will start collecting data about your website visitors. You can check this by going to GA4 > Reports > Realtime to monitor activity on your store. Congratulations, you’ve successfully integrated Google Analytics 4 with Magento 2!

Additionally, you can debug GA4 to ensure that data is being received correctly by your property.

Although it may seem more complex than Universal Analytics, you’ll come to appreciate its many benefits. GA4 allows you to track nearly any customer action on your store and use this data for dynamic remarketing campaigns or conversion tracking.

How to Set Up GA4 in Magento 2: A DIY Guide

Advantages of DIY Setup

The Power of Full Control

When it comes to setting up Google Analytics 4 (GA4) for your Magento 2 store, taking the DIY approach offers unparalleled flexibility. By handling the implementation yourself, you gain the ability to customize tracking events and configurations precisely to your business needs. This level of control allows you to capture data that truly matters to your e-commerce operations, ensuring you’re making decisions based on the most relevant insights.

Cost-Effective Solution

One of the most attractive aspects of a DIY GA4 setup is its cost-effectiveness. By foregoing expensive extensions, you can allocate your budget to other areas of your business. This approach is particularly beneficial for small to medium-sized enterprises looking to maximize their resources while still leveraging powerful analytics tools.

Step-by-Step Implementation

  1. Create Your GA4 Property Begin by setting up a new GA4 property in your Google Analytics account. Once created, you’ll receive a unique tracking code that forms the foundation of your implementation.
  2. Integrate the Tracking Code With your tracking code in hand, the next step is to add it to your Magento 2 store. This typically involves inserting the code snippet into the header or footer of your site’s template files. Careful placement ensures that the code fires on every page, providing comprehensive data collection.
  3. Configure Enhanced eCommerce Tracking GA4’s Enhanced eCommerce features offer deep insights into your customers’ shopping behavior. Setting this up manually requires a thorough understanding of Magento 2’s data layer and how to map its events to GA4’s parameters. While more complex, this step is crucial for capturing valuable metrics like product views, add-to-carts, and purchase data.
  4. Implement Custom Events To truly harness the power of GA4, setting up custom events is essential. This can be achieved through Google Tag Manager or by writing custom JavaScript code. Custom events allow you to track specific user interactions unique to your store, providing a wealth of actionable data.

Navigating Challenges and Mitigating Risks

Technical Hurdles

The DIY approach to GA4 setup demands a certain level of technical proficiency. You’ll need a solid understanding of JavaScript, familiarity with Magento 2’s architecture, and knowledge of how data layers function. This technical barrier can be significant for those without a background in web development or analytics implementation.

Time Investment

Compared to using a pre-built extension, manually setting up GA4 is undoubtedly more time-consuming. The process involves careful planning, meticulous implementation, and thorough testing. Be prepared to dedicate significant hours to ensure everything is configured correctly and functioning as intended.

Accuracy Concerns

Perhaps the most critical risk in a DIY setup is the potential for errors. Misconfigurations can lead to inaccurate data collection or, worse, complete data loss. This risk underscores the importance of thorough testing and validation throughout the setup process. It’s crucial to verify that all events are firing correctly and that the data flowing into GA4 aligns with your expectations.

Comparing the Two Approaches: Extension vs. DIY

Cost Considerations: Balancing Budget and Resources

When evaluating GA4 setup options for your Magento 2 store, cost is often a primary concern. Extensions typically come with an upfront price tag, ranging from modest to significant depending on the features and support offered. While this represents an immediate expense, it’s important to weigh it against the hidden costs of a DIY approach.

The DIY method may seem free at first glance, but it requires a substantial investment of time and potentially specialized skills. For businesses without in-house expertise, this could mean hiring a developer or consultant, quickly offsetting any initial savings. Consider your team’s capabilities and the value of their time when making this decision.

Time and Effort: Quick Wins vs. Deep Dives

Speed of implementation is where extensions truly shine. With most well-designed extensions, you can have GA4 up and running on your Magento 2 store in a matter of hours, if not minutes. This plug-and-play approach is ideal for businesses looking to quickly gather data and start making informed decisions.

In contrast, the DIY route demands a significant time investment. From understanding GA4’s intricacies to mapping out your tracking strategy and implementing custom code, the process can span days or even weeks. However, this time spent can lead to a deeper understanding of your analytics setup, potentially yielding more tailored insights in the long run.

Customization and Flexibility: Pre-built Solutions vs. Tailor-made Tracking

Extensions offer a convenient, out-of-the-box solution that covers most common e-commerce tracking needs. They often come pre-configured with events for key actions like product views, add-to-carts, and purchases. While this standardization ensures you’re capturing essential data, it may not account for unique aspects of your business model or customer journey.

The DIY approach, while more challenging, provides unlimited flexibility. You have the freedom to create custom events, set up complex tracking scenarios, and integrate GA4 data with other systems in ways that may not be possible with an extension. This level of customization can be particularly valuable for businesses with unique offerings or complex sales processes.

Long-Term Maintenance: Automatic Updates vs. Manual Control

Consider the long-term implications of your choice when it comes to maintaining and updating your GA4 setup. Extensions typically offer the advantage of automatic updates, ensuring your tracking remains compatible with the latest versions of both Magento 2 and GA4. This can save significant time and reduce the risk of tracking errors due to outdated code.

The DIY approach requires more vigilance. As GA4 evolves and Magento 2 releases updates, you’ll need to manually review and potentially modify your tracking code. While this demands more ongoing attention, it also allows for immediate adjustments and optimizations as your tracking needs change.

Ultimately, the choice between using an extension and going the DIY route for GA4 setup in Magento 2 depends on your specific circumstances. Consider your budget, available resources, technical expertise, and desire for customization. For many businesses, the convenience and reliability of an extension provide the best value. However, for those seeking granular control and willing to invest the time, the DIY approach can yield a highly tailored analytics solution.

Which Option is Best for Your Magento 2 Store?

Choosing the Right GA4 Implementation for Your Magento 2 Store

When to Choose an Extension

For many Magento 2 store owners, opting for an extension to implement Google Analytics 4 (GA4) can be the most straightforward and efficient choice. This approach shines in several scenarios:

Quick Setup, Minimal Hassle

If time is of the essence and you’re looking to get GA4 up and running with minimal fuss, an extension is your best bet. These plug-and-play solutions are designed for easy installation, often requiring just a few clicks to integrate GA4 with your Magento 2 store. This rapid deployment means you can start collecting valuable data almost immediately, without the need for extensive technical knowledge or coding skills.

Support When You Need It

One of the key advantages of choosing a reputable extension is the access to customer support. If you encounter any issues during setup or have questions about optimizing your tracking, you’ll have a dedicated team to turn to for help. This can be particularly reassuring for store owners who may not have in-house technical expertise to troubleshoot problems.

Advanced Features at Your Fingertips

Many GA4 extensions for Magento 2 come packed with advanced features that go beyond basic tracking. These might include enhanced e-commerce tracking, custom event setups, or integrations with other marketing tools. By leveraging these pre-built functionalities, you can access sophisticated analytics capabilities without the need to develop them from scratch.

When to Choose DIY

While extensions offer convenience, the DIY method of implementing GA4 in Magento 2 has its own set of compelling advantages for certain users.

Full Control for Tech-Savvy Owners

If you’re a developer or a store owner with strong coding skills, the DIY approach puts you in the driver’s seat. This method allows you to tailor every aspect of your GA4 implementation to your specific needs. From customizing event tracking to fine-tuning data collection, you have the freedom to create a bespoke analytics setup that aligns perfectly with your business goals.

Granular Customization

For stores with unique requirements or niche business models, the ability to customize tracking at a granular level can be invaluable. DIY implementation allows you to create custom events and parameters that capture data specific to your store’s unique customer journey or product offerings. This level of customization can provide insights that off-the-shelf solutions might miss.

Cost-Effective for Smaller Operations

If you’re running a smaller Magento 2 store or operating on a tight budget, the DIY approach can be particularly cost-effective. By leveraging your own skills or those of your team, you can implement GA4 without the ongoing costs associated with premium extensions. This can be especially beneficial for startups or small businesses looking to maximize their analytics capabilities while minimizing expenses.

Tailored Solutions for Specific Needs

Some stores may have very specific tracking requirements that aren’t easily met by existing extensions. In such cases, a DIY implementation allows you to build exactly what you need. Whether it’s integrating GA4 with custom checkout processes or tracking complex user interactions, you have the flexibility to create a solution that fits your unique use case.

Making the Right Choice

Ultimately, the decision between using an extension or opting for a DIY approach to GA4 implementation in Magento 2 depends on your specific circumstances. Consider your technical capabilities, time constraints, budget, and the complexity of your tracking needs.
